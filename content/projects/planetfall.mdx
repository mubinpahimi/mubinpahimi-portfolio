# Unmoderated & Moderated Testing
**Case study**  
Published on November, 2023 ¬∑ 5 min read  

As part of the PRISM+ website improvement initiative, I conducted both unmoderated usability testing and moderated UX interviews to uncover user pain points, assess task success, and validate design assumptions. These two research approaches complemented each other.  

[View Project](https://prismplus.sg)

---

## üß≠ Objectives
1. Evaluate the current PRISM+ website‚Äôs shopping experience and identify usability issues.  
2. Compare real user behavior (unmoderated) vs. internal stakeholder expectations (moderated).  
3. Validate task flows, content clarity, and decision points related to key PDP features.  
4. Build a structured research workflow for future usability testing rounds.  

## üß± Methods Used

### Unmoderated Usability Testing (General Users)
**Purpose:** Gather natural, unbiased user behavior on the live PRISM+ site.  

**Setup:**  
1. Conducted online using an unmoderated testing tool  
2. Duration: 20‚Äì25 minutes per participant  
3. Participants completed 6 tasks related to browsing, comparing, and purchasing PRISM+ products  
4. Tasks covered PDP navigation, collection browsing, cart interactions, and promotional understanding

![](/prismplus1.png)

**Key Deliverables:**  
- Task scenarios and success criteria  
- User behavior recordings  
- A quantitative scorecard for: Task completion, Usability score, Frustration level 

![](/prismplus2.png)

**Insights Uncovered:**  
- Natural friction points in live shopping flows  
- Real browsing hierarchy and information-seeking patterns  
- Pages where users struggled to make confident decisions  
- Input for navigation, PDP improvements, and content clarity  

### üåç Moderated Usability Testing (Internal Stakeholders)
**Purpose:** Deep-dive into usability issues with people who understand the ecosystem‚ÄîBD, CS, Marketing, Ops, Developers  

**Setup:**  
1. 1-on-1 interviews conducted via Google Meet  
2. Duration: 30‚Äì60 minutes each  
3. Booking process handled using Calendly  
4. Dates scheduled: 28 July ‚Äì 6 August  
5. Participants shared screens while performing tasks  

**Prepared Materials:**  
- Updated UX Interview Prep Kit (script, structure)  
- Refined participant tasks  
- Usability scorecard for task success + frustration  
- Probing questions tailored to stakeholder-specific insights  

**Why Moderated?**  
- Ask ‚ÄúWhy?‚Äù in real time  
- Clarify reasoning behind each decision  
- Identify internal expectations vs. actual user behavior  
- Gather suggestions from people who understand business constraints

## üß† Analysis: Affinity Mapping & Synthesis
After all moderated sessions, I performed Affinity Mapping to categorize findings across themes:  
- Navigation challenges  
- PDP clarity issues  
- Checkout friction  
- Misalignment between business expectations and user behavior  
- Content hierarchy gaps  
- Visual clutter vs. decision-making  

This resulted in clear problem clusters, allowing the team to prioritize fixes. I also documented all interview sessions, including timestamps, pain points, notable quotes, and recommendations.

---

## üìä Key Findings
**From Unmoderated Testing:**  
- Users struggled with inconsistent PDP sections  
- Some bundle information was unclear during comparison  
- Mobile experience showed gaps due to viewport issues  
- Users often missed secondary features like installation, warranty, and specs  

**From Moderated Interviews:**  
- Stakeholders highlighted gaps in product differentiation  
- Internal teams wanted better clarity for promotions (e.g., Climate Voucher)  
- Strong insights on content prioritization and hierarchy  
- Identified mismatches between internal assumptions and user expectations
